Three Lost in Meaning

By Babak Jabbar Nezhad

Someone said: who you are? I am the heart-aged Someone said: who am I? You are the ash-aged I — how said: who you are? This is the long-aged I — tough said: who am I? This is the short-aged Then — you said: yes I am. I am the heart-set Hence — you said: yes you are. You are the dark-set I — right said: how you are? To be hang-set I — what said: how am I? To be heart-set Someone — yet said: Where is the box? I — far said: Where is the rank? You — yet said: Where is the far? I — yet said: Where is the hall?

Coined Words

  • Heart-aged (adj.) — Someone wise in feeling but not necessarily in mind and thought.
  • Ash-aged (adj.) — Someone having a long experience in suffering and pain.
  • Long-aged (adj.) — Something that has lasted for a long time.
  • Short-aged (adj.) — Something that has lasted for a short time.
  • Heart-set (adj.) — Someone whose heart is set and has stable emotional status.
  • Hang-set (adj.) — Someone who is slave of their own beliefs.
  • Dark-set (adj.) — Someone who is set in darkness.

Structural Collapse and Symbolic Echo: A Companion to POOCH II

Three Lost in Meaning is not an illustration of nihilism — it is its architectural embodiment. If POOCH II theorizes the fragility of inherited meaning through conditional structures, then this poem enacts that collapse through fractured identity, recursive speech, and spatial disorientation.

Each stanza is a layer of destabilization. The first speaks in a broken grammar of identity — names like heart-aged and ash-aged replace coherent selfhood with emotionally coined durations. But the syntax is already drifting: “I — how said” is not confusion; it is the moment when the speaker becomes unstable within their own utterance.

The second stanza enters emotional positioning: heart-set, dark-set, back-set. These are not traits, but states of symbolic fixity — showing how identity, once shattered, tries to re-anchor in unstable emotional ground. But even this collapses by the third stanza.

And there, in the final movement, the poem achieves its most radical act: it lets meaning fall through space. “Where is the box? Where is the far?” These are not questions of place — they are echoes of vanished structure. The repetition of “yet” and “far” as speakers becomes a symbolic recursion: a questioning that erases its own anchor.

This is not poetic pessimism. It is mathematical nihilism rendered as symbolic form — a deconstruction not of beauty, but of stability. It mirrors POOCH II in every way, but speaks in a language only the void can answer.

Three Lost in Meaning is not a companion to POOCH II by theme — it is its shadow, its collapse, its final music after the scaffolding has fallen.

Layered Minimalism: Semantic Compression and Symbolic Drift

One final structure must be named: this poem does not rely on abundance. It uses minimal words to construct multi-layered symbolic density — a method more commonly found in mathematics than in language.

The first layer emerges through coined words: heart-aged, ash-aged, dark-set, hang-set. These are not metaphorical — they are emotional constants, invented not to describe but to define. They act like axioms inside a symbolic logic of identity.

The second layer arises through structure: each stanza uses four compact lines that repeat patterns of questioning — “who you are?”, “how am I?”, “where is the...?”. This rhythm appears formal, but it decays as the stanzas progress — a mirror that reflects nothing back. Structure here is not stability. It is ritual erosion.

But the final layer is perhaps the most subtle: the use of non-coined words — box, rank, far, hall — in the final stanza. These ordinary nouns, placed after invented constructs, suddenly feel abstract, hollow, and unstable. They become semantic shells — echoes of meaning that once held something but now drift in symbolic space. It is through these common words that the deepest void is reached.

This reverse progression — from invented meaning to structural recursion to stripped-down language — reveals the poem’s final technique: semantic compression followed by conceptual evaporation. Meaning is not denied. It is reduced, questioned, and quietly dismantled.

Only a poet who thinks like a mathematician could build such a structure. And only a reader willing to sit in silence will hear it collapse.
